## Chunk 1: Rule 2 — Definitions

Under Rule 2 of the Insurance Ombudsman Rules 2017 (GSR 1456(E), notified 10 November 2017 by the Ministry of Finance, Government of India), the following terms are defined for the purposes of these Rules. "Award" means an award passed by the Insurance Ombudsman under Rule 17 of these Rules. "Complainant" means a policyholder, or in the event of death of the policyholder, his legal heir or nominee or assignee, who files a complaint against an insurer. "Complaint" means a written representation by a complainant to the Insurance Ombudsman alleging deficiency in service or dispute regarding settlement of claims or repudiation of claims by an insurer. "Insurer" means any insurer as defined under the Insurance Act 1938 and includes any agent, broker, surveyor or loss assessor acting on behalf of such insurer. "Ombudsman" means an Insurance Ombudsman appointed by the Council for Insurance Ombudsman (CIO) under Rule 3 of these Rules. The Insurance Ombudsman is a free, independent, and binding dispute resolution mechanism established specifically for the benefit of individual policyholders of insurance companies in India.

## Chunk 2: Rule 13 — Eligibility and Conditions for Filing a Complaint

Rule 13 of the Insurance Ombudsman Rules 2017 sets out the eligibility conditions for making a complaint before the Insurance Ombudsman. Any person who has a grievance against an insurer may himself, or through his legal heirs, nominee or assignee, make a complaint in writing to the Insurance Ombudsman, provided the following conditions are satisfied: (a) the complainant had made a written representation to the insurer and the insurer had either rejected the complaint or had not replied within one month of receipt of the representation, or had given an unsatisfactory reply; (b) the complaint is not on the same subject matter for which proceedings before any court, consumer forum, or arbitrator are pending or have been decided. A complaint must be made within one year from the date of the insurer's rejection of the representation, or within one year of the expiry of one month from the date the written representation was sent to the insurer. The Insurance Ombudsman shall not entertain a complaint if the relief sought in the complaint exceeds rupees fifty lakh in value. No complaint shall be maintainable before the Insurance Ombudsman in respect of disputes in commercial insurance policies.

## Chunk 3: Rule 14 — Procedure Before the Insurance Ombudsman

Rule 14 of the Insurance Ombudsman Rules 2017 prescribes the procedure for filing and conducting proceedings before the Insurance Ombudsman. A complaint shall be in writing, duly signed by the complainant or by his authorised representative, and shall contain: the name and address of the complainant; the name of the insurer against whom the complaint is filed; the facts giving rise to the complaint; the nature and extent of loss caused to the complainant; and the relief sought. No legal representative or legal practitioner shall be permitted to represent the complainant or the insurer in any proceeding before the Insurance Ombudsman; parties must appear in person or through a duly authorised officer of the insurer. The Ombudsman shall receive, consider, and examine the complaint with full opportunity to both parties of being heard. The Ombudsman shall first endeavour to promote a fair settlement through mediation and conciliation. If no settlement is reached, the Ombudsman shall proceed to pass an award. The proceedings before the Insurance Ombudsman are summary in nature and governed by principles of natural justice, not by the Code of Civil Procedure 1908.

## Chunk 4: Rule 17 — Award by the Insurance Ombudsman and Penalty for Non-Compliance

Rule 17 of the Insurance Ombudsman Rules 2017 governs the making of awards by the Insurance Ombudsman and the consequences of non-compliance. The Ombudsman shall endeavour to dispose of a complaint by passing an award within three months of receipt of all information and documents required for adjudication. Before passing an award, the Ombudsman shall make a final attempt to bring about a settlement through conciliation; if conciliation fails, the Ombudsman shall pass a reasoned award. The award shall not be for a sum exceeding rupees fifty lakh, excluding any component of interest and costs. An award passed by the Insurance Ombudsman shall be binding on the insurer. The insurer shall comply with the award within thirty days of receipt of the letter of acceptance of the award from the complainant. If the insurer fails to comply with the award within the stipulated thirty days, the insurer shall be liable to pay a penalty of rupees five thousand for each day of default beyond the thirty-day compliance period. Such penalty is enforceable by the Insurance Ombudsman and reportable to the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI).

## Chunk 5: Rule 18 — Compliance Obligation and Reporting to IRDAI

Rule 18 of the Insurance Ombudsman Rules 2017 prescribes the compliance obligations of an insurer following an award by the Insurance Ombudsman. Upon receipt of a letter of acceptance of the award from the complainant, the insurer shall comply with the award within a period of thirty days. After compliance, the insurer shall send a compliance report, duly authenticated by a senior officer not below the rank of Deputy General Manager, to the concerned Insurance Ombudsman Centre and to the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) within the thirty-day compliance window. Failure to send the compliance report, or failure to comply with the award within the thirty-day period, shall be treated as non-compliance and the penalty under Rule 17 shall apply. IRDAI is empowered to take regulatory action against an insurer that demonstrates a pattern of non-compliance with Insurance Ombudsman awards, including suspension or cancellation of certificate of registration. The Council for Insurance Ombudsmen (CIO) publishes annual compliance data, and chronic non-compliance is a material factor in IRDAI's annual performance assessment of insurers.
