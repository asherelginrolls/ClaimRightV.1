## Chunk 1: Cashless Authorization Within One Hour — Mandatory Timeline (verbatim)

The IRDAI Master Circular on Health Insurance Business dated 29 May 2024 mandates strict timelines for cashless claims. Approval for Cashless facility: "a. Every insurer shall strive to achieve 100% cashless claim settlement in a time bound manner. The insurers shall endeavor to ensure that the instances of claims being settled through reimbursement are at bare minimum and only in exceptional circumstances. b. Insurer shall decide on the request for cashless authorization immediately but not more than one hour of receipt of request. Necessary systems and procedures shall be put in place by the Insurer immediately and not later than 31st July, 2024." A cashless pre-authorization request left undecided beyond one hour of receipt is a direct breach of this binding timeline. The one-hour cashless authorization rule applies to every insurer and every network hospital request, including emergency admissions.

## Chunk 2: Discharge Authorization Within Three Hours — Insurer Bears Delay Costs (verbatim)

The IRDAI Master Circular on Health Insurance Business dated 29 May 2024 provides, under "Final authorization for Discharge from the hospital": "a. Insurer shall grant final authorization within three hours of the receipt of discharge authorization request from the hospital. In no case, the policyholder shall be made to wait to be discharged from the Hospital. b. If there is any delay beyond three hours, the additional amount if any charged by the hospital shall be borne by the insurer from shareholder's fund. c. In the event of the death of the policyholder during the treatment, the insurer shall: i. immediately process the request for claim settlement. ii. get the mortal remains (dead body) released from the hospital immediately." Any hospital charges accruing because the insurer took longer than three hours to authorize discharge are payable by the insurer itself, from shareholders' funds — they cannot be passed to the policyholder.

## Chunk 3: Insurer and TPA Must Collect Claim Documents From the Hospital — Policyholder Not Required to Submit (verbatim)

The IRDAI Master Circular on Health Insurance Business dated 29 May 2024 places the burden of collecting claim documents on the insurer, not the policyholder. Under paragraph 17 (claims settlement): "b) In case, the claim is repudiated or disallowed partially, details shall be conveyed to the claimant along with full details giving reference to the specific terms and conditions of the policy document. c) Pursuant to intimation of the claim, Insurers and Third Party Administrators (TPAs) shall collect the required documents from the Hospitals. Policyholder shall not be required to submit the documents." Repeated or piecemeal document demands made to the policyholder for hospital-held records (discharge summary, itemised bills, treatment notes) contravene this provision: the insurer/TPA is obligated to source those documents from the hospital directly. A repudiation must also cite the specific policy terms and conditions relied upon — a bare or unreasoned rejection is non-compliant.

## Chunk 4: No Claim Repudiation Without PMC or CRC Approval (verbatim)

The IRDAI Master Circular on Health Insurance Business dated 29 May 2024 provides, under "Settlement of Claims": "a) No claim shall be repudiated without the approval of PMC or a three-member sub-group of PMC called the Claims Review Committee (CRC)." The Product Management Committee (PMC) comprises the Appointed Actuary, Chief Marketing/Distribution Officer, Chief Investment Officer, Chief Technology Officer, and Chief Compliance Officer of the insurer. A health insurance claim repudiation issued without the documented approval of the insurer's PMC or Claims Review Committee is procedurally invalid under the Master Circular. A policyholder may demand confirmation that the repudiation of their claim was reviewed and approved by the PMC or CRC, and the date of such approval.

## Chunk 5: 60-Month Moratorium — Policy and Claim Cannot Be Contested (verbatim)

The IRDAI Master Circular on Health Insurance Business dated 29 May 2024 provides, under "Policy/Claim cannot be contested": "No policy and claim of health insurance shall be contestable on any grounds of non-disclosure and/or misrepresentation except for established fraud, after the completion of the Moratorium Period, i.e. 60 months of continuous coverage. Note: The accrued credits gained under the ported and migrated policies shall be counted for the purpose of calculating the Moratorium period." After sixty months of continuous coverage — including credit for ported and migrated policies — an insurer cannot repudiate a health insurance claim on grounds of non-disclosure or misrepresentation of a pre-existing disease unless it establishes fraud. The moratorium is measured across ported policies, so switching insurers under IRDAI portability norms does not reset the sixty-month clock.

## Chunk 6: Ombudsman Award — 30-Day Compliance and ₹5,000/Day Penalty (verbatim)

The IRDAI Master Circular on Health Insurance Business dated 29 May 2024 provides, under paragraph 20, "Implementation of Ombudsman Award": "The Insurer is required to comply with the award of the Insurance Ombudsman within 30 days of receipt of award by the Insurer. In case the Insurer does not honour the ombudsman award, a penalty of Rs. 5000/- per day shall be payable to the complainant. Such penalty is in addition to the penal interest liable to be paid by the Insurer under The Insurance Ombudsman Rules, 2017." An insurer that fails to implement an Insurance Ombudsman award within thirty days owes the complainant ₹5,000 for every day of delay, over and above penal interest under the Insurance Ombudsman Rules 2017. This gives ombudsman awards real enforcement teeth for health insurance policyholders.

## Chunk 7: Renewal Cannot Be Denied for Claims History; No Fresh Underwriting at Renewal (verbatim)

The IRDAI Master Circular on Health Insurance Business dated 29 May 2024 protects renewal rights: "a. A health insurance policy shall be renewable provided the product is not withdrawn, except in case of established fraud or non-disclosure or misrepresentation by the Insured. b. An Insurer shall not deny the renewal on the ground that the policyholder had made a claim(s) in the preceding policy years. c. An Insurer shall not resort to fresh underwriting unless there is an increase in sum insured." Grace period: "The grace period of fifteen days (where premium is paid on a monthly instalments) and thirty days (where premium is paid in quarterly/half-yearly/annual instalments) is available on the premium due date. If the policy is renewed during grace period, all the credits (sum insured, No Claim Bonus, Specific Waiting periods, waiting periods for pre-existing diseases, Moratorium period etc.) accrued under the policy shall be" retained. Renewal within the grace period preserves all accrued waiting-period and moratorium credits.
