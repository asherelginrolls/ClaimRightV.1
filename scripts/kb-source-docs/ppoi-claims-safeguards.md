## Chunk 1: No Claim Shall Be Rejected or Closed for Want of Documents (verbatim)

The IRDAI Master Circular on Protection of Policyholders' Interests dated 5 September 2024 provides, in its claims provisions: "No claim shall be rejected or closed for want of documents or for delayed intimation of claim." This is a categorical safeguard: a health insurance claim cannot be repudiated, rejected, or closed on the ground that documents are missing or incomplete, nor because the claim was intimated late. Where documents are genuinely required, the insurer must pursue them (for hospital records, the insurer/TPA must collect them from the hospital under the Health Insurance Master Circular) — document gaps justify follow-up, never rejection or closure. A rejection letter citing "non-submission of documents", "incomplete documentation", or "delayed intimation" as the ground of repudiation is on its face contrary to this binding provision.

## Chunk 2: Claim Settlement Turnaround Times and Suo-Moto Delay Interest at Bank Rate Plus 2 Percent (verbatim)

The IRDAI Master Circular on Protection of Policyholders' Interests dated 5 September 2024 prescribes, under "Processing of claim and Turn Around Time (TAT) for settlement of claims": "Claim intimation received by the insurers shall be processed and settled within timelines specified as under: 1. Death claim, except in cases warranting investigation — Within 15 days from the date of intimation of claim. 2. Death claim warranting investigation — Within 45 days from the date of intimation of claim. 3. Surrender, partial withdrawal — Within 7 days of receipt of request. 4. Maturity Benefits, Survival benefits, Annuity payouts, Income benefits etc. — On due date." And critically: "In case the claim is not settled within the specified timelines, then the claimant is entitled for interest at bank rate plus 2 percent from the date of receipt of intimation till the date of payment. Such interest shall be paid by the insurers suo-moto along with the claim amount." Delay interest at bank rate plus 2 percent is automatic — the insurer must pay it on its own motion (suo-moto), without the claimant having to demand it. For health insurance cashless timelines (one hour pre-authorization, three hours discharge), see the Master Circular on Health Insurance Business dated 29 May 2024.

## Chunk 3: Information Must Be Sought In One Go, Not Piecemeal (verbatim, proposal stage)

The IRDAI Master Circular on Protection of Policyholders' Interests dated 5 September 2024 prohibits piecemeal information demands at the proposal stage: "In case of requirement of further details/clarifications on details given in the proposal form, the same should be called for in one-go and not on piecemeal basis within 7 days from the date of receipt of the proposal form." This one-go principle — all requirements must be communicated together, promptly, not in successive rounds — reflects IRDAI's consistent position against piecemeal demands on policyholders. At the claims stage, the same protection is achieved by two provisions: "No claim shall be rejected or closed for want of documents or for delayed intimation of claim" (this Master Circular), and the requirement that insurers and TPAs collect claim documents directly from hospitals so that the "Policyholder shall not be required to submit the documents" (Master Circular on Health Insurance Business, 29 May 2024). Successive, repeated document demands culminating in a rejection for incomplete documentation are inconsistent with this regulatory scheme.

## Chunk 4: Free Look Period and Refund With Penal Interest (verbatim)

The IRDAI Master Circular on Protection of Policyholders' Interests dated 5 September 2024 provides a free look period of 30 days from receipt of the policy document for policies with a term of one year or more, during which the policyholder may cancel if not satisfied with the terms. On free look cancellation, the insurer must refund "within 7 days of receipt of request for free look cancellation. In case of any delay in refund, the insurer shall: a) refund such amounts along with interest at the bank rate plus 2 percent on the refundable amount, from the date of receipt of the request for free look cancellation till the date of refund. This penal interest is without prejudice to any other penalty that may be levied by the Authority for non-compliance. b) Such interest shall be paid suo-moto by the insurer." Policy documents must be furnished within 15 days of acceptance of a proposal, including the covering letter informing the free look period, the policy document, and a copy of the proposal form.
